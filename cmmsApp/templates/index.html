Reliable Ring Main Unit (RMU) Solutions for Medium Voltage Networks

A Ring Main Unit (RMU) cabinet, also known as a medium voltage cabinet, is an integrated piece of electrical equipment designed to connect, measure, protect, and control medium voltage power distribution. It brings together fixed-type breakers and transformer protection in a compact, factory-assembled enclosure, improving the reliability and safety of your network.

RMU cabinets are engineered to enhance the performance of power distribution networks while offering advanced protection for transformers and other critical electrical assets. With modular construction and configurable busbar sections, RMUs are simple to install, maintain, replace, and expand as your system grows. They are available in both indoor and outdoor configurations, making them ideal for industrial, commercial, and utility applications.

  • Compact, fully enclosed MV cabinet with modular design
  • Voltage range typically between 7.2 kV to 36 kV
  • Integrated transformer and feeder protection with advanced switching
  • Indoor and outdoor variants with easy maintenance and future expansion

Explore RMU Types

ACB (Air Circuit Breaker) RMU

ACB-based Ring Main Units use Air Circuit Breakers for protection and control of medium voltage feeders and transformers. They provide reliable protection against overcurrent, short circuits, and earth faults, and are well suited for industrial and commercial installations with higher current ratings.

VCB (Vacuum Circuit Breaker) RMU

VCB-based RMUs extinguish the arc inside a sealed vacuum interrupter, delivering fast fault clearance, high reliability, and minimal maintenance. They are commonly deployed in urban and underground distribution networks where space is limited and uptime is critical.

SF6 Circuit Breaker RMU

SF6 RMUs use sulfur hexafluoride gas as the interrupting medium, offering high dielectric strength, compact footprint, and excellent arc-quenching capabilities. Ideal for medium-voltage networks, especially where high fault levels need to be handled in confined spaces.

Load Break Switch (LBS) RMU

LBS-type RMUs are equipped with load break switches instead of full circuit breakers. They are designed to switch and isolate circuits under normal load conditions and are a cost-effective option for rural or lower-demand distribution networks where fault levels are relatively low.

Combined Type RMU (ACB / VCB / SF6)

Combined RMUs integrate more than one type of interrupter technology (ACB, VCB, SF6) within a single assembly. This provides flexible protection strategies and allows different feeders or transformers to be protected according to their duty and criticality.

RMU Supply Process

Ring Main Unit (RMU) Supply Process

At iEngineering, we provide reliable medium voltage RMU solutions through a complete end-to-end process. We start by understanding your network configuration and site requirements, then prepare detailed designs, SLDs, and technical specifications to meet utility and project standards. Each RMU is manufactured and tested before dispatch, with on-site support for installation, SAT, and commissioning to ensure safe operation and seamless integration with your electrical system.

View Projects

  1. Consultation

    We review your project, SLD, and site needs to propose the most suitable and cost-effective RMU configuration for your network.

  2. Design, Drawing & Manufacturing

    Detailed design, GA drawings, and technical documentation are prepared. RMUs are then manufactured using certified components to meet the relevant MV standards and utility requirements.

  3. Factory Acceptance Test (FAT)

    Each RMU undergoes FAT, including wiring checks, insulation resistance, interlock operation, and functional testing. Clients may attend online or in person.

  4. Delivery

    Safe, on-time delivery of RMUs across Australia and the Pacific, complete with documentation, test reports, and lifting/handling instructions.

  5. Installation

    We provide guidance or supervision for RMU installation, ensuring that cable terminations, earthing, and clearances comply with drawings and safety standards.

  6. Site Acceptance Test (SAT)

    On-site checks for wiring, insulation, interlocks, and operation, with final adjustments as needed prior to energisation.

  7. Commissioning

    Final energisation, protection testing, and system integration ensure your RMUs are fully operational and delivering reliable power distribution.
